  • Our Latest Episode  


    • Why a Podcast?


    As a student organization dedicated to fostering knowledge and growth in the field of Product Management, we are launching our very own podcast on this fascinating subject. Our podcast will serve as a platform to bring you insightful discussions, expert interviews, and valuable perspectives on all things related to Product Management.

    Well, we believe that podcasts are an incredibly powerful medium for sharing knowledge and facilitating meaningful conversations. They offer a convenient and accessible way to consume content, whether you're commuting, exercising, or simply taking a moment to relax. With our podcast, we aim to provide you with valuable insights and inspiration that you can enjoy anytime, anywhere.

    Our Podcasts' Series.

    Episode I

    What is Product Management?

    Listen to this episode on our Podcast on Spotify. In this episode, Dr. Vasant Gondhalekar joins me to discuss the ins and outs of Product Management. We cover the definition of PM, the key responsibilities of a product manager, and the necessary skills and qualities for success in this role.

    Episode II

    Why Product Management?

    In this episode, I am joined by Chase Rivera, a Senior Consultant/Product Manager at Deloitte, as we explore why many new product managers who he interviews, fail to answer the question "Why do you want to become a product manager?" properly, and how to give an impressive response.

    Episode III

    Part I: The "Dark Side" of being a PM

    In this episode, I am joined by Tiffany Chen, MBA Lead Product Manager at AT&T, and Alexis Guel, Product Manager at Indeed. In this episode, I'll be stating 7 potential dark sides (or challenges) of being a product manager, and both Tiffany and Alexis will validate them based on their experience.


    Episode IV

    Part II: The "Dark Side" of being a PM

    In the Part II of the "dark sides" of being a Product Manager, I am joined by Alexis, a Product Manager from Indeed. Alexis gives a fresh perspective on the challenges of being a PM, sharing similarities but also huge differences from that of Tiffany's, on part I. This shows how Product Management role differs from one company to another
